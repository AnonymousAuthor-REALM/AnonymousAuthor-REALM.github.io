REALM: Reasoning Any 3D Scenes with LVLM

Anonymous Authors
Submitted to AAAI 2026
Paper (Coming Soon) arXiv (Coming Soon) Code Repository

REALM leverages the knowledge of an LVLM to perform open-vocabulary 3D semantic segmentation.

Abstract

Enabling AI systems to reason like humans is crucial for effective interaction in virtual 3D world. Although recent approaches support natural language-based interaction with 3D scenes, they remain limited in understanding implicit user intentions. The advent of Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs) has shown great promise in achieving robust visual-language understanding. Inspired by this, we propose \textbf{REALM}, a unified LVLM-guided 3D world reasoning framework that enables diverse interaction capabilities, including segmentation, object removal, replacement, and style transfer. Given a user instruction, with the assist of LVLM, REALM autonomously interprets the underlying intent, identifies the most appropriate operation from REALM-toolkit, and applies it to the target object in the 3D scene accordingly.

3D Reasoning Segmentation Results

Visualization of the 3D reasoning segmentation results of our method REALM and previous methods. When faced with implicit instructions, REALM accurately identifies the target 3D objects.

The local refinement is designed to improve the quality of the output. It refines the complete 3D object mask in just 50 steps.
